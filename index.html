Augmented Reality Quiz

ABSTRACT

The AR Quiz using Open CV and Gesture Recognition is a UI-based application for students and teachers to easily make and use an Augmented Reality system. AR as a platform is just now reaching its full potential. Since smartphones and mobile devices are now at a sufficiently large user base, it is worth looking at the potential for an extremely small form factor delivery system that is flexible, easily modified, and can be used by educators and students. An easily modifiable AR learning experience will present an AR Mobile platform development, interactive museums, and the chosen subject in a new style. The Quizzes in this project can be shown to improve not only knowledge of the chosen subject through investigation, but a better understanding of the development potentials and knowledge of the mobile devices now ubiquitous to students. In the AR Quiz using Open CV and Gesture Recognition, the user can log in and select a quiz in which he/she wants to examine his/her intelligence.... When the camera opens, depending on the quiz, and what type of questions are there in the database, the camera will show the first question and its UI will appear on the screen as an overlay that is, the image will be superimposed on the background and the questions appear on the screen randomly. The Hand Gesture Recognition system works in the background and it captures all the hand movements and detects all the gestures and movements of the finger coordinates. When the user selects an option using two fingers, the option gets selected and if the answer is correct, the score is added to the database of the user. There can be different user profiles and admin profiles. The user can launch and use the UI-based application. The administration system can add more questions of different categories which can be administered by the admin. It will be very user interactive and hence will be found interesting by the students to learn in this way. The use of Augmented reality and Headsets in future will be more common for gaming, entertainment and education purposes as the Metaverse is gaining popularity these days. It is useful for students who want to learn new things like children in Play-school and Kindergarten. The children will be very much curious to learn new things in this way.

Augmented Reality (AR) is a combination of a real and a computer-generated or virtual world. It is achieved by augmenting computer-generated images in the real world. Augmented reality is the integration of digital information with the user's environment in real-time. Unlike virtual reality (VR), which creates a totally artificial environment, AR users experience a real-world environment with generated perceptual information overlaid on top of it. It is of four types namely marker-based, markerless, projection based and superimposition-based augmented reality. Augmented reality comes under the field of mixed reality. It can be considered as an inverse reflection of Virtual Reality.

Scope of Augmented Reality
Augmented Reality has the potential to be used in almost every industry, including:
1. Education:
Augmented Reality is replacing traditional methods and introducing creative ways to learn. AR motivates students to engage, interact and increase the ability of students to learn curricular subjects.
2. Military:
The military has been devising uses for augmented reality for decades. The idea here is that an augmented-reality system could provide troops with vital information about their surroundings, such as showing where entrances are on the opposite end of a building, somewhat like X-ray vision.
3. GamingAugmented reality is a combination of game visuals along with the audio content with respect to the user environment in real-time which helps in the establishment of an artificial environment.AR is used to create a playing zone within the existing artificial environment.
4. Repair & MaintenanceWhether it's a car motor or an MRI machine, repair and maintenance staff are beginning to use AR headsets and glasses while they provide useful information on the spot, suggest potential fixes, and point out potential trouble areas.

Hand gesture recognition on Python and OpenCV by Ahmad Paul Ismail

Hand gesture recognition is one of the systems that can detect the gesture of a hand in a real-time video. The gesture of hand is classified within a certain area of interest. This project concentrates on how a system could detect, recognize and interpret hand gesture recognition through computer vision with the challenging factors which are variability in the pose, orientation, location and scale. To perform well in developing this project, different types of gestures such as numbers and sign languages are created in the system.
